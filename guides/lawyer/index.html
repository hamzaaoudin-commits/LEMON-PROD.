AI & the Lawyer.

AI now drafts, researches, and maps precedent at a speed no associate can match. The risk to your career isn't the model — it's the lawyer next door who repriced their work around it first.

What stays irreplaceable

Judgment under uncertainty

Deciding what advice to give when the law is ambiguous and the stakes are real — and standing behind it.

Reading true intent

Hearing what a client actually wants behind what they say, and what they're not telling you.

Owning the risk

Putting your name, your bar number, and your liability on a recommendation. A model can't be accountable.

What AI absorbs now

Case research

Pulling relevant precedent at scale is now near-instant and near-free.

First-draft synthesis

Memos, summaries, and first-pass contracts are produced in seconds, not billable hours.

Document review

Triage of large document sets is increasingly an automated, supervised task.

The hybrid move

Where to stand instead.

01

Stop billing for synthesis

Let AI produce the raw material you used to bill hours for, and bill instead for the judgment on top of it.

02

Sell the decision, not the document

Your client doesn't want a 12-page memo — they want to know what to do. Package the answer.

03

Specialize faster

Use the time AI frees to go deeper in a niche where accountability and relationships compound.

The 90-day plan

Start this quarter.

  1. 01Audit your last 20 invoices: flag every line item AI can now produce in minutes.
  2. 02Re-price one service from hourly to a fixed fee anchored on outcome, not effort.
  3. 03Build a model-assisted first-draft workflow you supervise, so output speed stops being your bottleneck.
  4. 04Pick the one practice area where your judgment and reputation are hardest to copy, and concentrate there.

Lawyers who'll be fine in five years already know which part of the job to stop defending — and which to double down on.
