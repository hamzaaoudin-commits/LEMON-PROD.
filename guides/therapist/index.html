AI & the Therapist.

Notes, scheduling, and even first-line support chatbots are spreading. But the core of therapy — a real human holding another's distress — is the hardest thing to automate.

What stays irreplaceable

Human presence

Being truly with someone in pain — the mechanism of the work itself.

Relational attunement

Reading what's beneath the words, session over session.

Ethical containment

Holding risk and responsibility for another person's wellbeing.

What AI absorbs now

Notes and admin

Documentation and scheduling are automating.

Psychoeducation

General information and coping content is freely generated.

First-line support

Low-acuity check-ins are moving to apps.

The hybrid move

Where to stand instead.

01

Outsource the desk

Let AI handle admin so you're fully present in the room.

02

Lead with the relationship

Make the human bond your explicit, irreplaceable value.

03

Use tools between sessions

Position apps as support around your work, not a substitute.

The 90-day plan

Start this quarter.

  1. 01Automate notes and scheduling to protect your presence in session.
  2. 02Articulate clearly why the relationship is the treatment, to clients and referrers.
  3. 03Decide where tech supports your work and where it must never replace you.
  4. 04Build a practice known for depth, not volume.

Apps can inform. They can't sit with someone in the dark. That's still your job.
