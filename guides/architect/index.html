AI & the Architect.

Generative tools now produce floor plans, variants, and renders in minutes. The architect's value moves decisively from drafting to vision, judgment, and accountability.

What stays irreplaceable

Design vision

Knowing which of a thousand generated options is actually good, and why.

Code and context judgment

Navigating regulation, site, and client reality into something buildable.

Stewardship of the project

Owning the outcome from sketch to handover — a chain a model can't carry.

What AI absorbs now

Drafting and documentation

Production drawings are increasingly automated from intent.

Option generation

Variants and massing studies are now near-instant.

Rendering

Photoreal visualization is a fast, cheap commodity.

The hybrid move

Where to stand instead.

01

Curate, don't draft

Use generation to explore widely, then sell the taste that selects.

02

Lead the project, not the file

Position yourself as the judgment across the whole build, not the drafter.

03

Charge for vision

Decouple your fee from drawing hours and tie it to design outcomes.

The 90-day plan

Start this quarter.

  1. 01Integrate generative tools into early-stage exploration to widen your option space.
  2. 02Reframe client conversations around vision and outcome, not deliverable count.
  3. 03Build a portfolio that shows judgment and taste, not production volume.
  4. 04Identify the project phases where your accountability is irreplaceable and price them.

Generating options is free now. Knowing which one is right is the whole job.
