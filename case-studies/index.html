Case studies

The method, applied.

Worked examples of the approach — how we find the one irreplaceable facet of a profession and cut a repositioning around it. These are illustrative demonstrations of the method, not client claims.

Worked exampleN°01

Lawyer

The situationAI now drafts, researches, and maps precedent at a speed no associate can match. The risk to your career isn't the model — it's the lawyer next door who repriced their work around it first.

The irreplaceable facet

Judgment under uncertainty

Deciding what advice to give when the law is ambiguous and the stakes are real — and standing behind it.

The repositioning move

Stop billing for synthesis

Let AI produce the raw material you used to bill hours for, and bill instead for the judgment on top of it.

Worked exampleN°02

Copywriter

The situationDrafting words is now free, instant, and infinite. Selling words by the hour or the page is a race to zero. The copywriters who survive sell what the model can't: taste and strategy.

The irreplaceable facet

Taste

Knowing which of fifty drafts actually lands — and killing the other forty-nine.

The repositioning move

Stop selling words

Sell the strategy and the editorial judgment; let the model produce volume.

Worked exampleN°03

Real Estate Agent

The situationListing copy, pricing estimates, and buyer-property matching are becoming automated and free. The agents who survive sell the parts of the deal a model can't touch.

The irreplaceable facet

Negotiation under pressure

Reading the room and closing a deal when emotions and money collide.

The repositioning move

Stop competing on access

Listings are public. Compete on the negotiation and the read on the deal.
